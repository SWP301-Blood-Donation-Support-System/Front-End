Eligibility Requirements

Most people can donate blood. Here are some basic requirements:

  • Be at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some states)
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds
  • Be in good general health and feeling well
  • No tattoos or piercings within the past 3 months
  • Have not donated blood in the last 56 days

Common Reasons People Cannot Donate

Low Iron Levels

If your hemoglobin level is too low, you may need to wait before donating blood.

Recent Travel

Travel to certain countries may temporarily disqualify you from donating.

Certain Medications

Some medications may require a waiting period before donation is possible.

Recent Illness

You should be feeling well and healthy on the day of donation.

Types of Blood Donations

Whole Blood

The most common type of donation where we collect one pint of whole blood.

  • Time: About 1 hour
  • Frequency: Every 56 days
  • Best For: All blood types

Power Red

Donate red cells only while other blood components are returned to you.

  • Time: About 1.5 hours
  • Frequency: Every 112 days
  • Best For: Types O, A-, B-

Platelet

Donate platelets, which help blood clot and are needed for many medical procedures.

  • Time: About 2.5-3 hours
  • Frequency: Every 7 days (up to 24 times/year)
  • Best For: Types A, B, AB

Plasma

Donate plasma, which helps maintain blood pressure and supplies critical proteins.

  • Time: About 1.5 hours
  • Frequency: Every 28 days
  • Best For: Types AB, AB+

Frequently Asked Questions

Most donors report only a brief pinch when the needle is inserted. Throughout the donation process, you should experience minimal discomfort. Our trained staff is skilled at ensuring your donation experience is as comfortable as possible.

The entire process takes about one hour from start to finish. The actual blood donation typically takes 8-10 minutes, but you should allow for registration, health history, mini-physical, and refreshment time after donation.

For a whole blood donation, approximately one pint (about 500 ml) is collected. The average adult has between 8-12 pints of blood in their body, so one pint is only a small portion. Your body replaces the fluid in hours and the red blood cells within weeks.

In most states, you must wait 3 months after getting a tattoo or piercing before donating blood if the procedure was done in a state-regulated facility with sterile needles. If the tattoo or piercing was done in a non-regulated facility, a 3-month waiting period is required.

Before donating: Eat a healthy meal, drink plenty of fluids, and get a good night's sleep. Avoid fatty foods before donating as they can affect blood tests.

After donating: Drink extra fluids, avoid strenuous activity for 12 hours, keep the bandage on for at least 4 hours, and eat a healthy meal. If you feel dizzy, lie down with your feet elevated.
