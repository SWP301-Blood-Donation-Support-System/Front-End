Eligibility Requirements

General Eligibility Guidelines

To ensure the safety of both donors and recipients, we have certain eligibility requirements that must be met before donating blood. These requirements include:

Basic Requirements

  • Be at least 17 years old in most states (16 years old with parental consent in some states)
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds
  • Be in good general health and feeling well on the day of donation
  • Have a valid ID

Health-Related Eligibility Factors

Various health conditions and medications may affect your eligibility to donate blood. Here are some common considerations:

Most medications do not prevent you from donating blood. However, some medications may require a waiting period after your last dose. Common examples include:

  • Accutane, Absorica, Amnesteem, Claravis, Myorisan, Sotret, or Zenatane (isotretinoin) – 1 month
  • Avodart, Jalyn, or dutasteride – 6 months
  • Blood thinners (e.g., Coumadin, Warfilone, Jantoven, warfarin) – 7 days
  • Hepatitis B Immune Globulin – 12 months

Some medical conditions may temporarily or permanently prevent you from donating blood. These include:

  • Anemia
  • Cancer
  • Heart disease
  • HIV/AIDS
  • Hepatitis B or C
  • Uncontrolled diabetes

Please consult with our staff if you have any medical conditions or concerns.

Some recent procedures may temporarily delay your ability to donate:

  • Dental work – 24 hours after minor procedures, 3 days after major procedures
  • Surgery – depends on the type, typically 6 weeks to 6 months
  • Tattoos – eligible if applied in a state-regulated facility with sterile needles and ink
  • Piercings – 3 months if not done with single-use equipment

Travel-Related Eligibility

Travel to certain regions of the world may temporarily defer your eligibility to donate blood due to potential exposure to malaria, Zika virus, or other infectious diseases.

Travel Deferrals

Common travel deferrals include:

  • Travel to malaria-endemic areas – typically a 3-month deferral
  • Travel to areas with Zika virus outbreaks – typically a 1-month deferral
  • Extended stays in certain European countries (risk of variant CJD) – may result in indefinite deferral

The Day of Donation

On the day of your donation, please:

  • Eat a healthy meal before donating
  • Drink plenty of non-alcoholic fluids
  • Bring a valid ID
  • Wear comfortable clothing with sleeves that can be raised above the elbow
  • Get a good night's sleep before donating

Note: This information is provided as a general guide. Eligibility requirements may change over time. The final determination will be made by health professionals at the time of donation.
